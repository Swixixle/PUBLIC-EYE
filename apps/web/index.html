    What the record shows

    On August 7, 2022, Manchin voted for the Inflation Reduction Act — the largest climate investment in US history. He cast the deciding vote. He also negotiated the bill's final text. The methane fee on oil and gas producers, present in earlier versions, was removed. Section 50265, which requires the Interior Department to offer oil and gas leases as a precondition for permitting renewable energy on federal land, was added.

    These are not allegations. They are numbered provisions in a public law. The question this receipt answers is not whether Manchin supported or opposed climate action. It is whether his claim — that he never took a dime from the fossil fuel industry — is accurate.
